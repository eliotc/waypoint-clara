## HECS-HELP (for Commonwealth-supported domestic students)

### What is HECS-HELP?
HECS-HELP is a federal government loan scheme that allows eligible domestic students to defer their tuition fees. You pay nothing upfront — the debt is repaid through the tax system once your income exceeds the repayment threshold.

### Am I eligible?
To access HECS-HELP you must:
- Be an Australian citizen, or a New Zealand citizen/permanent resident meeting residency conditions
- Be enrolled in a Commonwealth-supported place (CSP)
- Submit a valid Tax File Number (TFN) to the university before the census date

### Census Date
The census date is the last date to withdraw from a subject without incurring a HECS-HELP debt. Census dates are typically:
- **Semester 1**: Last Friday of March
- **Semester 2**: Last Friday of August

### Repayment Thresholds (2026–27)
You repay your HECS-HELP debt via the ATO when your income exceeds **$54,435** per year. Repayments are a percentage of your income, starting at 1% and increasing up to 10% for very high incomes. The debt is indexed to CPI each June.

## International Student Fees
International students pay full tuition fees (no HECS-HELP access). Annual fees for international students are approximately **20–40% higher** than domestic fees. Contact international@kingsford.edu.au for a personalised fee schedule.

- **Pay upfront**: 10% discount on the student contribution amount (domestic HECS students only)

- **Single annual payment**: Preferred by international students on fixed budgets
