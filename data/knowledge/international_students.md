# International Students at Kingsford University

## Why Study at Kingsford?
Kingsford University welcomes students from over 90 countries. Melbourne is consistently ranked one of the world's most liveable cities, and Kingsford's diverse campus reflects that — nearly 30% of our student body comes from outside Australia.

## Student Visa (Subclass 500)

### What is it?
The Student visa (subclass 500) allows international students to study full-time in Australia for the duration of their course. It is issued by the Australian Department of Home Affairs, not the university.

### How to get it
1. Receive an offer of admission from Kingsford
2. Accept your offer and pay the deposit (usually one semester's tuition)
3. Kingsford issues a **Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE)**
4. Apply for your Student visa at immi.homeaffairs.gov.au using your CoE
5. Receive your visa decision (usually within 4–6 weeks of a complete application)

### Visa conditions
- Must study full-time (typically 4 subjects per semester)
- Must maintain satisfactory academic progress
- Can work up to **48 hours per fortnight** during semester (unlimited during official university breaks)
- Dependents (spouse, children) may be included on the visa

### Genuine Student requirement
Your visa application must demonstrate you are a Genuine Student — you intend to study, not use education as a migration pathway. The university's Admissions team can advise on how to strengthen your application.

## English Language Requirements
| Test | Minimum Score |
|---|---|
| IELTS Academic | Overall 6.5 (no band below 6.0) |
| TOEFL iBT | Overall 79 (writing 21, speaking 18) |
| PTE Academic | Overall 58 (no skill below 50) |
| Cambridge C1 Advanced | 176 overall |

If you don't meet these requirements, you may be eligible for the **Kingsford English Language Program (KELP)**:
- 10-week program: achieves approximately 0.5 IELTS band gain
- 20-week program: approximately 1.0 IELTS band gain
- Pathway directly into undergraduate study upon successful completion

## Tuition Fees and Payments
International students pay full (non-HECS) tuition fees. Indicative annual fees:
- Engineering & Technology: AUD $19,000–$24,000
- Business & Commerce: AUD $18,000–$22,000
- Health Sciences: AUD $20,000–$26,000
- Arts & Humanities: AUD $16,000–$19,000

Fees are payable per semester. A **10% deposit** (typically one semester's fee) is required to secure your Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE).

## Scholarships for International Students
The **Kingsford International Student Scholarship** provides AUD $7,000 per year off tuition fees for eligible high-achieving students. Applications close 30 September 2026 for 2027 entry. See the Scholarships section or ask Clara for more details.

## Arrival and Orientation
- Kingsford offers an **Airport Pickup Service** for new international students arriving in January/February (bookable for a $80 fee via the International Centre)
- **International Student Orientation** is a dedicated 2-day program before O-Week, covering Australian culture, transport, banking, mobile plans, and academic expectations
- A **buddy program** matches new international students with returning students from similar backgrounds

## On-Campus Support
- **International Student Advisers**: Dedicated team for visa, accommodation, and wellbeing queries — international@kingsford.edu.au
- **Peer Mentors**: Trained current students who can answer practical day-to-day questions
- **Multicultural Student Services**: Cultural events, cooking nights, celebration of national holidays
- **Prayer and Reflection Rooms**: Quiet, non-denominational spaces available across campus

## Healthcare
International students must hold **Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC)** for the duration of their visa. Kingsford arranges OSHC through Bupa as part of the enrolment process. Cost is approximately AUD $700–$900/year. The on-campus Medical Centre bulk-bills OSHC holders for standard GP consultations.

## Banking and Tax File Number
- Open an Australian bank account before you arrive (ANZ, CommBank, and NAB all offer student accounts with no monthly fees and can be opened from overseas)
- Apply for a **Tax File Number (TFN)** through the ATO website once you arrive — you'll need this to work legally in Australia
- Kingsford's International Centre runs a "First Steps in Australia" workshop covering banking, TFN, and mobile plans in Week 1

## Working in Australia
- Students on a subclass 500 visa can work up to **48 hours per fortnight** during semester and unlimited hours during university breaks
- The current minimum wage is AUD $24.10/hour (2026 rate). Hospitality, retail, and campus jobs are popular with students
- Kingsford's Career Centre maintains a job board with part-time and casual roles suited to international students

## Post-Study Pathways
After graduating, international students may be eligible for a **Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485)**, allowing 2–6 years of work in Australia depending on their qualification and study location. Engineering, Health Sciences, and regional study may attract additional points for permanent residency pathways. Consult a registered migration agent for personalised advice — Kingsford's International Centre can provide referrals.

## The International Centre

The **International Centre** is Kingsford's dedicated hub for global students, located on **Level G of the Student Centre** — the glass-fronted building at the heart of the main campus, directly opposite the South Lawn.

### What's inside
- **Welcome Lounge**: A large open lounge with comfortable seating, international newspapers, and a rotating display of student artwork from around the world. This is where new students come on their first day.
- **Adviser Rooms**: Private meeting rooms for one-on-one consultations with International Student Advisers on visa conditions, enrolment, and wellbeing.
- **Document Certification Desk**: Certified copies of passports, transcripts, and visa documents for enrolment and rental applications.
- **Prayer and Reflection Room**: A quiet, non-denominational space on the ground floor, open 7am–9pm daily.
- **Noticeboards**: Weekly listings of housing, part-time jobs, language exchange partners, and community events.

### Airport Pickup and Arrival
The International Centre coordinates the **Airport Pickup Service** for students arriving in January and February. A Kingsford staff member or volunteer meets you at the international terminal with a welcome sign and drives you to campus or your accommodation. Cost: $80, bookable through the International Centre portal.

### Peer Mentor drop-in
Trained Peer Mentors (current international students in their second year or above) hold drop-in sessions in the Welcome Lounge every Monday and Thursday 11am–1pm. They can answer practical questions that advisers can't — like the best cheap grocery stores nearby, how to open a bank account without a fixed address, or which buses run late at night.
