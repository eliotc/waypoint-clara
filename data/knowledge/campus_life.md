# Campus Life at Kingsford University

## Our Campus
Kingsford University's main campus is located on Kingsford Boulevard in Melbourne's inner-north, just 4km from the CBD. The campus spans 28 hectares and includes five faculty precincts, a central library, student hub, sports complex, and three cafés.

## Accommodation

### On-Campus Residences
Kingsford has two on-campus residential colleges:
- **Parkview College**: Traditional college experience with shared dining and a close-knit community. ~$260/week for a single room (meals included).
- **Kingsford Apartments**: Self-contained studio and one-bedroom apartments for students who prefer independence. ~$320/week.

Both residences are 5 minutes' walk from lecture theatres. Priority is given to first-year students from regional or international backgrounds.

**How to apply**: Apply for residence at the same time as your course application. Demand is high — apply early. Contact residence@kingsford.edu.au.

### Off-Campus Living
Melbourne has a large rental market. Popular suburbs for Kingsford students include:
- **Fitzroy / Collingwood**: Vibrant, close to campus, avg. $350–$450/week for a shared house
- **Brunswick**: Student-friendly, good tram links, avg. $280–$380/week shared
- **Carlton**: 15 min tram ride, historically popular with students, avg. $320–$420/week

The Student Housing Service can connect you with housemate boards and listings at housing.kingsford.edu.au.

## Transport
- **Tram**: Route 19 stops directly at the campus main gate (runs every 6 minutes peak hour)
- **Train**: 10-minute walk from Brunswick Station (Upfield line)
- **Bike**: Dedicated cycle paths connect the campus to the city; secure bike storage on campus
- **Car**: Limited permit parking available ($800/year). Not recommended — Melbourne CBD traffic is challenging

Students with a valid student card pay concession fares on all Melbourne public transport (~$2.00 per trip, $10.60 daily cap).

## Student Clubs and Societies
There are over 80 registered student clubs at Kingsford, including:
- **Academic societies** (CS Society, Medical Students Association, Law Review)
- **Cultural clubs** (Chinese Students Association, Indian Cultural Society, African Students Network)
- **Sport** (AFL, netball, basketball, rock climbing, esports)
- **Creative** (Film Society, Photography Club, Student Theatre)
- **Advocacy** (Student Environment Collective, Disability Student Network, LGBTQ+ Alliance)

New students are encouraged to attend O-Week (Orientation Week) at the start of Semester 1 to explore clubs and meet other students.

## Sports and Fitness
The Kingsford Sports Complex includes:
- 25-metre indoor swimming pool (open 6am–9pm weekdays)
- Fully equipped gym with 200+ stations ($150/semester, free for scholarship holders)
- Basketball and netball courts
- Outdoor athletics track
- Yoga and group fitness studio

Kingsford competes in the University Sport Victoria (USV) competition across 12 sports.

## Library and Study Spaces
The **Kingsford Central Library** is open 24/7 during semester and holds over 800,000 physical and digital resources. Key services:
- Quiet study zones, collaborative spaces, and individual study rooms (bookable online)
- 3D printers, recording studios, and maker space in the Digital Innovation Lab
- Research consultations with specialist librarians (free, bookable)

## Student Services
- **Counselling**: Free, confidential mental health counselling. 10 sessions per year, same-week appointments available in urgent cases.
- **Medical Centre**: On-campus GP clinic with bulk billing for Medicare card holders
- **Disability and Accessibility**: Note-taking support, exam adjustments, assistive technology — contact access@kingsford.edu.au
- **Career Centre**: Resume reviews, mock interviews, job board, and annual careers fair (April and September)
- **Childcare**: On-campus childcare centre for students and staff with children aged 0–5 (waitlist applies)

## Food and Dining
- **The Hub Café**: Centrally located, open 7am–7pm weekdays. Good coffee, $10 daily specials.
- **Faculty Canteens**: Lower-cost options in Engineering and Health buildings
- **Foodhall**: International cuisine options, open for lunch and dinner
- **Vending**: 24/7 vending throughout campus

## O-Week (Orientation Week)
O-Week for Semester 1 2027 runs **24 February – 28 February 2027**. Activities include campus tours, faculty welcome sessions, club sign-ups, a night market, and the Big Day Out sports carnival. Attendance is strongly encouraged for all new students.
